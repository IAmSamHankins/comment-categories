"At the age of 82 my dad had Medicare part A and no other insurance. I begged him to sign up and pay for for part B. After a long and antagonistic argument he did so. Six months later my dad suffered from a perforated ulcer. This required hospitalization and a long course of follow up medical care. His part B insurance coverage paid for many thousands of dollars of expenses for which my dad otherwise would have been personally responsible.

I proudly told my dad that his part B insurance had been a great deal for him. “No”, my dad forcefully argued. He said that he had paid the part B premiums for six months before receiving any benefits. He instead should have delayed six months before signing up for part B and thus avoided paying premiums while he was in good health. My dad and I never again had a civil conversation. (He died at the age of 91.)

My dad’s idea and the Republican plan are to eschew insurance in favor of fee for service. But my dad could never have afforded the medical services he needed without insurance. I don’t get it, but if that’s what my fellow citizens want I ask just one thing. Let’s also repeal the Emergency Medical and Treatment Labor Act (EMTLA) passed by Congress in 1986, which explicitly forbids the denial of care to indigent or uninsured patients in emergency rooms based on a lack of ability to pay. If we are going to have fee for service, let’s do it cleanly and honestly.
